# Dementia Care Companion – Chapter Outline

This document outlines the planned chapters for the guide. Each chapter focuses on a practical, non‑medical aspect of supporting older adults with memory changes.

## 1. Adapting Communication
**Status:** ✅ Complete (merged)
- How to talk and listen with compassion
- Validating emotions, not correcting facts
- Using visual and non‑verbal cues
- Creating a calm communication environment

## 2. Creating a Supportive Environment
**Status:** ⏳ Planned
- Practical adaptations to the home to reduce confusion and anxiety
- Simplifying layouts, using clear labels, ensuring good lighting
- Framing changes as similar to making a home safer for someone with poor vision
- Reducing clutter and establishing familiar “anchor” objects

## 3. Finding Joy in Daily Routines
**Status:** ⏳ Planned
- Establishing simple, predictable daily rhythms that provide comfort
- Accessible activities: listening to music, gentle walking, looking at photo albums, simple gardening
- Emphasising enjoyment and connection over achievement
- Adapting activities as abilities change

## 4. Supporting the Caregiver
**Status:** ⏳ Planned
- Recognising and managing caregiver stress
- Building a support network
- Taking time for self‑care without guilt
- Navigating family dynamics and expectations

## 5. Navigating Emotional Changes
**Status:** ⏳ Planned
- Understanding that mood swings, anxiety, or withdrawal are common
- Responding with patience and empathy
- When to seek additional support (without medicalising the experience)
- Maintaining the person’s dignity and autonomy

---

This outline reflects the project’s philosophy: memory loss is a normal part of aging, and our focus is on compassionate care, not cure. Each chapter will be developed as a separate markdown file in the `guide/` directory.
